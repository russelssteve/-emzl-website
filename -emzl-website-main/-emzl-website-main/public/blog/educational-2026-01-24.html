Introduction

Many traders don't understand the fundamentals of risk management, which is why 90% of retail traders lose money. This guide will teach you the essential principles to protect your capital.

The Basics

Risk management is about controlling how much you can lose on any single trade or series of trades.

1. Position Sizing

Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade. If you have $1000, risk maximum $20 per trade.

2. Stop Loss Orders

Always set a stop loss before entering a trade. This limits your maximum loss.

3. Risk-Reward Ratio

Aim for at least 1:2 risk-reward ratio. If you risk $10, target $20 profit.
